## 1. What Octano is (and is not)

Octano is an **informational** app. It gathers and displays, in one place:

- **public and official** records from the ANP (Brazil's fuel supply inspection, retailer registry and weekly prices), reproduced as they appear in the source, with origin and base date;
- **public and official** records from state Procons and the national SINDEC/Senacon system (cited companies and substantiated complaints), likewise reproduced as they appear in the source. **An administrative citation or complaint does not mean a final court conviction** and may be under dispute — the app shows the case number, status, amount and resolution rate exactly as published;
- stations with pumps certified by IPEM-SP (the "Safe Pumps" seal), reproduced from the public source;
- location, ratings and reviews provided by **Google**;
- prices voluntarily reported by the **community of users**, always labeled as such.

Octano **does not inspect stations, does not certify fuel and does not make its own judgment about any establishment**. Seals, colors and summaries are visual organizations of public records, always attributed to the source.

## 2. Data matching and confidence level

Associating a station shown on the map (Google data) with an ANP, Procon or SINDEC record involves matching name, address, coordinates and CNPJ (company ID). The app **states the confidence level** of that match and, when it is not confirmed, shows the records as **"to confirm"** — without claiming they belong to the establishment. Verification tools (comparison with the CNPJ on your receipt, viewing the raw data and community validation) are available on the details screen.

Information is provided **"as is"**, with no warranty of accuracy, completeness or timeliness. **The decision to fuel up (or not) at any establishment is yours.** Always consult the official sources: [gov.br/anp](https://www.gov.br/anp).

## 4. Are you the owner or manager of a station?

If you identify incorrect data, a wrong match or a record that does not belong to your establishment, contact us through our channel at **[fontinelles.com/contact](https://fontinelles.com/contact)**, with the subject **"Data dispute"**, providing the station name in the app, the CNPJ and the problem. The case will be reviewed and, if a matching error is confirmed, the association will be corrected or removed in the next update. Correcting the **content** of the official records, however, is up to the source agency (ANP, Procon or IPEM) — the app only reproduces the public databases.
